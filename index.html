Executive Summary

The Marco Polo UN Pier Initiative represents a comprehensive maritime solution to overcome critical access constraints preventing adequate humanitarian aid delivery to Gaza's 2.1 million residents. This technical documentation presents a detailed operational framework for establishing a high-capacity offshore pier system capable of delivering 3,000+ metric tons of aid daily.

Critical Context: Current delivery mechanisms reach less than 10% of documented humanitarian need (62,000 MT/month required vs. 1,300 MT/month delivered). This documentation provides a technically viable, politically neutral pathway to circumvent bureaucratic impediments that have resulted in systematic aid denial.

This open-source technical framework invites collaboration from international maritime experts, humanitarian practitioners, and policy specialists to refine and implement life-saving infrastructure solutions that transcend political barriers to humanitarian access.
